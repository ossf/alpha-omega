## Package Repository Security

### Compromised Password Protection

RubyGems.org now detects compromised passwords on user sign in in production. Since deploying the feature, we have detected 600 compromised passwords during login, and 18 users have successfully updated their password.

## Infrastructure Security

Due to renewed concerns about a potential compromise of the RubyGems.org AWS infrastructure from the unauthorized server access in September 2025, we contracted with [Volexity](https://www.volexity.com/) to do a deep dive on our AWS infrastructure and supporting production services to specifically look for any evidence of tampering or compromise.  The report is expected to be delivered in early April.  

In March, the team made significant progress on the remaining operational fitness task across RubyGems.org, covering service reliability practices including defined SLIs/SLOs for critical service APIs, monitoring and dashboards, and linked runbooks. Incident management documentation was formalized with on-call procedures, severity definitions, post-mortem processes, and status page protocols, while deployment operations were hardened with docs covering rollback strategies, zero-downtime DB migrations, and deploy notifications. 

The team also addressed ongoing sustainability through Dependabot and community PR triage expectations, Ruby and Rails upgrade policies, and security and knowledge distribution practices to ensure no critical knowledge or credentials remain siloed. 

Disaster recovery work is scoped and will be addressed in a future cycle.
